Why is ML-KEM Overhead 37x Higher?

Legacy elliptic curve algorithms like **X25519** rely on modular arithmetic on small cyclic groups. Post-Quantum KEM algorithms (like **ML-KEM/Kyber**) are based on **Module Learning with Errors (MLWE)**, which operates on high-dimensional polynomial vectors.

Representing these large lattice vector structures requires thousands of coefficients to guarantee 128-bit quantum security, causing the public key (~1.2 KB) and ciphertext (~1.1 KB) to expand dramatically. This has huge implications for network packet fragmentation!
